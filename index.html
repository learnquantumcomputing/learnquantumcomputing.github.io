What is quantum computing?

Quantum computers use the phenomena of quantum physics and mechanics to perform computational operations. Unlike classical computers which perform computations with discrete binary states, quantum computers use superposition states of 0 or 1 to represent fundamental units of data. Superposition, along with entanglement and quantum inteference, make up the quantum phenomena which allow quantum computers to excel in cryptography, quantum simulation, optimization, and searching.

Why learn quantum computing?

Quantum computing is a rapidly-emerging technology. New advances in the technology are being made by universities and industry leaders. Within the next decade, there will be a greater demand for engineers and programmers in the quantum computing space.
