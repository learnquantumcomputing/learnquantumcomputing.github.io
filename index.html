What is quantum computing?

Quantum computers use the phenomena of quantum physics and mechanics to perform computational operations. Unlike classical computers which perform computations with discrete binary states, quantum computers use superposition states of 0 or 1 to represent fundamental units of data. Superposition, along with entanglement and quantum inteference, make up the quantum phenomena which allow quantum computers to excel in cryptography, quantum simulation, optimization, and searching.

Why learn quantum computing?

Quantum computing is a rapidly-emerging technology. Within the next decade, there will be a greater demand for engineers and programmers in the quantum computing space.

How to prepare for a career in quantum computing

Students learning quantum computing should be well-versed in physics, mathematics, and computer programming. Undergraduate students should major in either physics, computer science, or electrical engineering. Students should also aim for a PhD in their chosen field and pursue a thesis related to quantum computing.
